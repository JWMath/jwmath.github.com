Generalized Configuration space

In 2019, Professor Xuezhi zhao and I generalized Fadell's configuration space. We consider the subset M of vector space or projective space, defined generalized configuration space which is formed by n-tuples of elements in M where any k-elements of each n-tuple are linearly independent. Actually, this generalized configuration space also generalized Stiefel manifold. Based on this definition, we calculated its homotopy group, homology group, cohomology group. We also got some results on mapping from sphere to Euclid space.

The groups Gnk,Γnk

In 2015, Vassily O. Manturov formulated following fundamental principle:

If dynamical systems describing the motion of n particles posses a nice codimension one property governed by exactly k particles, then these dynamical systems admit a topological invariant valued in Gnk.

The first paper is: Non-Reidemeister Knot Theory and Its Applications in Dynamical Systems, Geometry, and Topology

V.O.Manturov, I.M.Nikonov, S.Kim, D.A.Fedoseev have done many works about this. Most of their work can be found in their book: On Groups Gnk and Γ nk: A Study of Manifolds, Dynamics, and Invariants

Borsuk-Ulam theorem and Knaster's problem

Knaster's problem: In 1947, B.Knaster posed the following question:

Given a continuous function f mapping the (n-1)-dimentional Euclidean sphere Sn-1 &sube Rn into Rm, m &le n-1, and k=n-m+1 points p1,...,pk ∈ Sn-1, does there exist a rotation r ∈ SO(n) such that f(r(p1))=...=f(r(pk))?
